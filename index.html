June 27, 1976
Entebbe, Uganda.
An Air France flight was hijacked and diverted to Entebbe. 106 Jewish and Israeli hostages were separated and held 4,000 km from Israel, with Idi Amin's full cooperation.

The hijackers demanded the release of 53 Palestinian prisoners. Deadline: 72 hours.

The Minister of Defense was Shimon Peres. He was 52 years old.
"We do not negotiate with terrorists — not because it is easy, but because the alternative destroys something we cannot rebuild."
Operation Thunderbolt · July 4, 1976
100 Israeli commandos landed at Entebbe at midnight. In 90 minutes, 102 of the 106 hostages were freed. Three hostages were killed in the crossfire. Commander Yonatan Netanyahu was killed — the only Israeli soldier to die. Dora Bloch, hospitalized before the raid, was murdered by Amin's forces afterward.

It was considered one of the most daring rescue operations in history.
